Srinivasa T. Reddy, Ph.D

Dr. Srinivasa Reddy received his Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry at Osmania University in Hyderabad, Telangana, India, and his Master’s degree in Biological Sciences at Roorkee University in Roorkee, Uttarakhand, India. He earned his PhD in Comparative Biochemistry at the University of California, Berkeley, and became a postdoctoral scholar at the University of California, Los Angeles for Cell and Molecular Biology.

He is presently a professor of Molecular and Medical Pharmacology for the Interdepartmental program of Molecular Toxicology.

Dr. Reddy is primarily interested in understanding the expression, regulation, and mechanism of action of enzymes involved in arachidonic acid metabolism. He researches their role in the development of inflammatory diseases with focus on discovering diagnostic and therapeutic molecules for their treatment.

Currently, his ongoing projects include
  1. The role of cyclooxygenase 2 (COX2) in the resolution of inflammation in IBD. His laboratory was the first to report that COX2 plays an anti-inflammatory role in the mouse intestine and established a novel mouse model of Crohn’s disease.
  2. The role of Paraoxonase 2 (PON2) in ischemia/reperfusion injury, heart failure, and obesity. His laboratory is conducting pioneering research on PON proteins. They have demonstrated that PON1 an HDL-associated enzyme and its cellular counterparts (PON2 and PON3) participate in the inactivation of inflammatory responses initiated by oxidized lipids.
  3. Development of apoA-I mimetic peptides for the treatment of inflammatory diseases: Over the last fifteen years, based on his work on oxidized lipids and HDL, he has developed a novel class of anti-inflammatory peptides. These agents called apoA-I mimetic peptides show promise for the treatment of inflammatory diseases. More recently, he has developed a novel therapeutic strategy by generating transgenic tomatoes expressing apoA-I mimetic peptides and is preparing to test these peptides in the clinic.
